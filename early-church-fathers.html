Early Church Fathers

The Early Church Fathers were theologians and writers who played a key role in the development of early Christian doctrine. Their writings provide valuable insight into how the New Testament was interpreted, transmitted, and used in the early centuries of Christianity. By quoting and referencing New Testament texts, they help scholars understand the history and authorship of the New Testament books.

Significance of the Early Church Fathers

The Early Church Fathers were key theologians and writers in early Christianity who helped shape the Christian tradition. They are significant to biblical studies because they frequently cited New Testament texts in their writings, often quoting them verbatim. These citations help scholars establish the authenticity and dating of New Testament books.

How Church Fathers Relate to Manuscripts

Church Fathers such as Origen, Irenaeus, and Augustine referenced New Testament texts in their works. These quotations allow scholars to compare them to surviving manuscripts, providing additional layers of evidence for dating manuscripts and determining which variants of the text may have existed in the early centuries.

Role in Dating and Authorship of Bible Books

The Church Fathers provide some of the earliest non-manuscript evidence for the dating and authorship of the New Testament. Their writings often affirm traditional authorship (such as the Gospels being attributed to Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John) and support the dating of books to the first century.
